\n\n'''Konya''' ({{IPA-tr|ˈkon.ja}}) is a major city in south-western edge of the [[Central Anatolia Region|Central Anatolian Plateau]] and is the [[List of cities in Turkey|seventh-most-populous]] city in [[Turkey]] with a metropolitan population of over 2.1 million. Konya is an economically and industrially developed city{{cite web|url=http://www.ft.com/intl/cms/s/db1d9cee-7650-11db-8284-0000779e2340.html|title=Financial Times: Reports — Anatolian tigers: Regions prove plentiful|website=Ft.com|accessdate=7 August 2018}} {{cite web|url=http://www.investopedia.com/terms/a/anatolian-tigers.asp|title=Anatolian Tigers|author=root|work=Investopedia|accessdate=25 May 2015}} {{Cite web |url=http://us.zaman.com.tr/us-tr/newsDetail_getNewsById.action?newsId=3660 |title=Zaman: Anatolian tigers conquering the world |access-date=2013-08-21 |archive-url=https://archive.is/20130821181544/http://us.zaman.com.tr/us-tr/newsDetail_getNewsById.action?newsId=3660 |archive-date=2013-08-21 |dead-url=yes |df= }} and the capital of [[Konya Province]].\n\nAfter its conquest by Turkish tribes, Konya became the capital of the [[Seljuq dynasty|Seljuk]] [[Sultanate of Rum|Sultanate of Rum (Anatolia)]] (1077–1308) and the [[Karamanids]] (13th century–1487).
